      Menstruation

      Menstruation also known as a period or monthly is the regular discharge of blood and mucosal tissue (known as menses) from the inner lining of the uterus through the vagina.During a normal menstrual cycle, the lining of a woman's uterus sheds. This cycle is part of a woman's reproductive system and prepares the body for a possible pregnancy.

      Normal Menstruation

      Age

      The first period usually begins between twelve and fifteen years of age,a point in time known as menarche.

      Frequency

      The typical length of time between the first day of one period and the first day of the next is 21 to 45 days in young women, and 21 to 31 days in adults (an average of 28 days)

      Normal Menses

      Duration

      Bleeding usually lasts around 2 to 7 days.

      Abnormal Menstruation

          Periods that occur less than 21 days or more than 35 days apart

          Missing three or more periods in a row

          leeding or spotting that happens between periods, after menopause or following sex

          Periods that are accompanied by pain, cramping, nausea or vomiting

          Menstrual flow that is much heavier or lighter than usual Periods that last longer than seven days
